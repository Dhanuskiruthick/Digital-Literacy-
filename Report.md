1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================

This project is submitted as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy
course at VIT Bhopal University. As a Student Digital Ambassador,
I was tasked with building a Digital Literacy Portfolio covering
five key areas: digital awareness, professional online presence,
coding platforms, email communication, and cybercrime awareness.
All tasks have been documented and uploaded to a single GitHub
repository. This report summarises my work, observations, and
reflections for each task.

================================================================
2. TASK 1 – DIGITAL LITERACY AWARENESS INFOGRAPHIC
================================================================

For this task, I used Canva (canva.com) to create a one-page
digital literacy infographic. My design covers three key topics:
what digital literacy means, safe internet practices for students,
and the importance of maintaining a professional online presence.
I chose Canva because it is free, browser-based, and easy to use
without any prior design experience.

The infographic uses a clean layout with icons, short bullet
points, and colour-coded sections to make it visually appealing
and easy to read. I found it challenging to fit all the
information into a single page while keeping it uncluttered.
However, this taught me an important lesson about concise
communication — a skill that is highly relevant in the digital
world. Overall, creating the infographic helped me understand
that digital literacy is not just about using technology, but
about using it responsibly and effectively.

================================================================
3. TASK 2 – STUDENT DIGITAL PORTFOLIO
================================================================

For this task, I set up professional profiles on three platforms:
LinkedIn, Kaggle, and ResearchGate. LinkedIn is a professional
networking platform used by students, recruiters, and industry
professionals worldwide. Kaggle is a data science and machine
learning platform where users can practise coding, explore
datasets, and participate in competitions. ResearchGate is an
academic networking platform where researchers and students can
share papers, follow ongoing research, and connect with scholars
across the world.

On LinkedIn, I filled in my Education section with my current
programme, institution, and expected graduation year. On Kaggle,
I set up a basic profile to begin exploring data science
resources. On ResearchGate, I created a profile to stay
connected with academic research relevant to my Cyber Security
programme.

Over the next four years, I plan to use LinkedIn to connect with
professionals and apply for internships, Kaggle to practise data
science and improve my coding skills, and ResearchGate to follow
the latest research in cybersecurity and contribute to academic
discussions as I progress in my studies.

4. TASK 3 – CODING AND COLLABORATION PLATFORMS
================================================================

For Part A of this task, I created an account on HackerRank and
completed a beginner-level coding challenge. This helped me get
comfortable with an online coding environment and understand how
competitive programming platforms work. The challenge required
me to write a simple program, which gave me confidence in
approaching coding problems systematically.

For Part B, I created a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness
Quiz using Google Forms. The quiz includes three multiple choice
questions and two short answer questions covering topics like
phishing, strong passwords, and safe internet habits. I also
linked the form to a Google Sheet to track responses
automatically.

These tools will help me academically — HackerRank for practising
coding before placements and internships, and Google Forms for

group projects during my course.

================================================================
5. TASK 4 – EMAIL ETIQUETTE
================================================================

For this task, I drafted two professional emails — one requesting
a deadline extension from a professor, and one expressing
interest in a summer internship opportunity. Writing these emails
made me realise how important tone, structure, and word choice
are in professional communication. A poorly written email can
create a very negative impression, even if the request itself
is reasonable.

One situation where poor digital communication caused a real
problem is when students send informal, unstructured messages
to professors or recruiters — using abbreviations like "plz" or
"u" or skipping subject lines entirely. This often leads to the
email being ignored or the student being perceived as
unprofessional. A simple habit of proofreading before sending
and always including a clear subject line can make a significant
difference to how one is perceived online.

================================================================
6. TASK 5 – CYBERCRIME AWARENESS
================================================================

For this task, I researched phishing attacks in India and wrote
a detailed case study based on a real 2024 report. What surprised
me most was the scale of the problem — over 1.35 lakh phishing
incidents were reported in just the first half of 2024, a 175%
increase from the previous year. I had assumed phishing was an
occasional problem, but it is clearly a massive and growing
threat, especially for new internet users like college students.

As a result of this research, one habit I will personally change
is to always check the URL of any website carefully before
entering my login credentials or OTP. I used to assume that if
a website looked genuine, it was safe — but I now understand
that attackers can clone websites almost perfectly. Verifying
the URL and looking for HTTPS is a simple but powerful habit
that can prevent financial loss.

================================================================
7. CONCLUSION
================================================================

This project gave me a practical introduction to the digital
skills that every student needs in today's world. I learned how
to build a professional online presence, communicate effectively
via email, use coding and collaboration platforms, and protect
myself from online threats. The most valuable takeaway for me
was understanding that digital literacy is an ongoing practice —
not a one-time activity. I will continue building on these
skills throughout my academic and professional journey.
