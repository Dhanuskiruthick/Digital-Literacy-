## General Safety

- [ ] Never click links in emails or WhatsApp messages asking you to 
      "verify" your account
- [ ] Always check the URL carefully before entering login details — 
      fake sites often have slight misspellings 
      (e.g., sbi-secure-login.com instead of sbi.co.in)
- [ ] Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts 
      — email, Instagram, and your college portal
- [ ] Never share your OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from 
      your bank or TRAI
- [ ] Use different passwords for different accounts — never reuse the 
      same password across platforms
- [ ] Download apps only from the official Play Store or App Store — 
      never from links received in messages or emails
- [ ] If a deal looks too good to be true (e.g., "You won an iPhone!"), 
      it is almost certainly a scam — ignore and report it

## UPI and Financial Safety

- [ ] Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger — scanning a QR code to 
      receive money is a myth, you only end up losing money
- [ ] Never enter your UPI PIN to "accept" a payment request — your PIN 
      is only required when you are sending money, never receiving it

## Reporting

- [ ] Report any cybercrime immediately at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- [ ] Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24x7)

*Always stay alert. When in doubt, don't click.*
