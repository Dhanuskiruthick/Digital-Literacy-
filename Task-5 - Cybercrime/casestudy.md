# Case Study: Phishing Attacks Targeting Indian Internet Users (2024)

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as 
trustworthy organizations — such as banks, government bodies, or popular 
apps — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, 
OTPs, or bank details.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The attacker creates a fake website or email that looks identical to a 
   real service (e.g., SBI, HDFC, or a government portal).
2. A message is sent via email, WhatsApp, or SMS saying "Your account will 
   be blocked — click here to verify."
3. The victim clicks the link and lands on the fake page.
4. They enter their login credentials, OTP, or card details.
5. The attacker captures this data instantly and uses it to steal money 
   or identity.

## Real Incident
Between January and June 2024, individuals and organisations in India faced 
over 1.35 lakh phishing attacks related to financial services — including 
banking, e-commerce, and payment platforms. This represented a 175% rise 
compared to the same period the previous year, driven by increased digital 
adoption and the use of AI by attackers to craft convincing fake content.
Phishing remains the most prevalent cyberattack vector in India, responsible 
for 22% of all cyber incidents in 2023.

Source: Business Standard / Kaspersky Report, November 2024
URL: https://www.business-standard.com

## Who Is Usually Targeted?
First-time internet users, college students, senior citizens, and anyone who 
recently started using UPI or online banking are the most common victims.

## Consequences
- Victims lose money directly from their bank accounts
- Personal data gets sold on the dark web
- Social media and email accounts get hijacked
- Victims may face identity theft and long-term financial damage
