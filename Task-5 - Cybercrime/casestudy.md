Case Study: Phishing Attacks Targeting Indian Internet Users (2024)
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy organizations — such as banks, government bodies, or popular apps — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details.
How it typically happens (step-by-step):

The attacker creates a fake website or email that looks identical to a real service (e.g., SBI, HDFC, or a government portal)
A message is sent via email, WhatsApp, or SMS saying "Your account will be blocked — click here to verify"
The victim clicks the link and lands on the fake page
They enter their login credentials, OTP, or card details
The attacker captures this data instantly and uses it to steal money or identity

Real Incident (Source): Between January and June 2024, organisations and individuals in India faced over 1.35 lakh phishing attacks related to financial matters — banking, e-commerce, and payment systems. This was a 175% rise compared to the same period the previous year, driven by increased digital adoption and the use of AI by attackers to create convincing fake content. Business Standard
Who is targeted: First-time internet users, college students, senior citizens, and anyone who recently started using UPI or online banking.
Consequences: Victims lose money, their personal data gets sold on the dark web, and their bank accounts or social media accounts get compromised. Phishing remains the most prevalent cyberattack vector in India, responsible for 22% of all cyber incidents in 2023. Corbado
Source: Business Standard / Kaspersky Report, November 2024 — business-standard.com
